Project Summary
For BEEP's Adventures, Environmental Threat, and the Mech AI Tournament, please click their image links above to learn more.

The below projects are smaller than those mentioned above and are examples of my experience, not my current capabilities, as each was a project that taught me new things. If I did any of them again, they would be of much higher quality. Nevertheless, please do check a few out if you are interested.
Gold Runner
Gold Runner is a quick minigame I developed to help build my Unreal Engine 4, C++, and Blueprints abilities. It is actually the second playable game I made in Unreal, with the first being a FPS where you shoot evil cubes, which will progress into "Project Laserhand."

The game simply involves the spawning of gold nugget pawns at random points above the map which, when picked up, speed up the character and soundtrack. The gold nuggets spawn increasingly fast over time at a fair rate and the player dies when they do not collect enough gold. The project used and adapted the Third Person Character template and was great fun to work with.
Widow Mirror
Play
Widow Mirror is a short JavaScript-based web game that is designed as advertisement material for the 2020 Black Widow Movie. I was challenged in a web class to make a JavaScript advertisement application, so I thought I would make a game.

The core mechanic of the game, the mirroring, is based on the copycat behaviour from the two widows in the movie's trailer. In my game, I turned this idea into a mechanic where you control both Black Widows at once and they mirror each other's movements, and you have to control both of them and fight enemies. There is simple collision mechanics, the widows can shoot each other, and there is even a boss battle.
Ruin 2112
Play
RUIN 2112 is a 2.5D side-scrolling SHMUP (shoot-'em-up) where you play as a robot who has come back from the future to rid the world of zombies after an apocalypse. It was made in Unity with C# and is completely custom apart from the visual assets and animations. It has a retro arcade-game feel with simple controls and snappy movement.

I made RUIN in about a week and I had a lot of fun with it. It was perfectly scoped, where the base of the game was increadibly simple and affective, which made it easy to keep adding quality of life content (such as powerups) until the deadline.
The Staff
The staff is a 3D fantasy-style short RPG where you play as an ice-and-fire mage and have to balance these elements. You can charge ice spells with left-click, and fire spells with right-click, where you cannot use one element too much as this will cause the staff to temporarily break. You fight pigmen enemies with AI senses and behaviour that detect bolts and hear their allies fighting or dying. There is even a boss pigman at the end of the dungeon.

The Staff is the only non-solo project on this page, where I worked with two designers in its creation. My contributions in this project involved creating all the code from the player staff mechanics to the enemy AI, creating custom FX for the spells, texturing and implementing the enemies, and building and fixing the map and lighting. It was experience working with a team member that contributed very little, and how to go about dealing with this. There are definitely many things I would have done differently with this project now that I have this experience.
Custom Shader Scene
As a part of my programming major, I learnt how to program shaders. I created a completely custom shader script in Unity that creates and recognises textures, normal maps, lighting, and shadows. The below image is a quick screencap of this 300+ line shader code.
This project gave me in-depth knowledge on how games render graphics and how the graphics pipeline can be manipulated. The left image is the result of these shaders, complete with many textured and normal-mapped models, an animated fire, billboarded trees, and moving lights.

Now with Unity's Shader Graph and Unreal's Material Editor, creating shaders is easy, and I can apply what I leant with this project to them.
ROB
To wrap up, ROB, or Raspberry-Pi One Button is a simple wireless controller I made in my first year. ROB was a Raspberry-Pi (a mini-computer) literally duct-taped to a battery and a breadboard. I wrote a python script to wirelessly connect the Pi to my computer, then ran a simple snake knock-off, with its button moving the snake.

This subject taught me the inner-workings of a computer, how to code circuitry, and how to think outside the box.
