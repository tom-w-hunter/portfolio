Environmental Threat
Educational mobile game made in Unity for Baringa State Primary School to educate its students about the endangered Wallum Sedge Frog. Our game became a part of their curriculum.

Role: Programmer in a group of 4 with a designer, an animator, and a producer.

Environmental Threat was built as three minigame levels: one to teach students how to identify the frog's habitat, one to teach how their habitat is being affected (changes in pH), and one to identify its calls.
Mech AI Tournament Champion
My "AI for Games" class of Semester 1 2020 ran a Mech AI tournament that I won. Its livestream can be found here.

This tournament involved using behaviour trees via PandaBT in Unity to run in-script behaviours programmed in C#. We were only able to change the robot's decisions, not their physical abilities. Our behaviour scripts were then attached to Mech prefabs and pitched against each other in a round-based tournament.

There were 24 participants from our class, and I made it to the semi-finals, then to the finals, and won the finals. My AI bot also beat the top 3 champions from 2019 in a bonus round.

My bot's success lies in my application of in-depth analysis and planning before writing any code, and the ruthless patching of bugs and discrepancies after writing code. BEEP-X had perfected weapon-centered heuristic functions that were the result of analysing each of the four available weapons (in terms of range, ammo, speed, firing angle). I played the game myself and devised the best strategies (camping pickups, rushing centre to get kills faster, healing at appropriate times, avoiding movements while shooting the beam weapon) and then implemented these strategies as behaviour to the best of my abilities. I used behaviour trees to my advantage by splitting its root into movement and shooting selectors that are run in parallel, so the bot could perform movement patterns and shooting behaviour simultaneously.

Creating this bot took me under two weeks and I fit its artificial brain in 500 lines of well-documented code and 7 key behaviours. I also submitted my code a week early. Overall, it was a fun experience to challenge myself and learn about behaviour trees and artificial decision making.
