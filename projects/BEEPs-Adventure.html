Project BEEP's Adventure

BEEP's Adventure was my first published game that I made in a studio. I developed this game with six of my classmates as an indie studio through both semesters of 2020. This project allowed me to develop and reinforce a wide range of Unity, game development, and timetabling skills.

Description
BEEP's Adventure is a 3D chaos puzzler where you play as the prototype box sorting robot B.E.E.P. (Box Efficiency Experiment Program) who was designed as one of Box Corp's many robots and machines to improve the efficiency of its factory. However, you have gone rogue and instead solve puzzles to cause as much destruction as possible in order to escape the factory.
Game Summary
BEEP's Adventure plays as a level-based task solver, where the player causes mischief and mayhem, while being directed towards progressing via a set of tasks in each level. It was inspired by other chaos task-solvers such as Untitled Goose Game.

The game has a tutorial level and two main levels. It was designed to have 15-20 minutes of quality playtime as the player progresses through the levels.

My Role
I played a programming role in the development of BEEP's Adventure with one other programmer/audio engineer, two designers, two animators, and a producer. We had little guidance from our peers aside from weekly assessed critiques and blogs. I completed and showcased an average of 14 hours of work per week, where this development was documented here for semester 1 2020 and here for semester 2.

My major contributions include:
  • Much of the player controls and actions.
  • Implementing all major animations.
  • Much of level implementation (object placement, lighting, collision).
  • Binary save data system.
  • Dynamic task list system.
  • Tutorial tooltip system.
  • Asynchronous level loading and lighting pre-load.
  • NPC Navmesh, behaviour, reactions, and AI.
  • Explosion FX and physics.
  • Level endings and credits.
