Project BEEP's Adventure

BEEP's Adventure is a 3D chaos puzzler where you play as the prototype box sorting robot B.E.E.P. (Box Efficiency Experiment Program) who was designed as one of Box Corp's many robots and machines to improve the efficiency of its factory. However, you have gone rogue and instead solve puzzles to cause as much destruction as possible.

Gameplay
My Role
BEEP's Adventure is a Unity game I worked on through both semesters of my final year. I played a programming role in a group of 7 with two designers, two animators, one producer, and one other programmer. The game was made in Unity with complete custom assets apart from sounds and fonts.

My contributions to the project involve half of its programming and Unity implementation. The work I put in equates to at least 340 hours, with around 14 hours per week for 24 weeks. A few of my contributions include:
  • Implementing all major animations.
  • Much of the player controls and actions.
  • Much of level implementation (object placement, lighting, collision).
  • Binary save data system.
  • Dynamic task list system.
  • Tutorial tooltip system.
  • NPC Navmesh, behaviour, reactions, and AI.
  • Level endings and transitions.
  • Explosion FX and physics.
Game Summary
BEEP's Adventure plays as a level-based task solver, where each task causes destruction and allows for progression, much like Untitled Goose Game.

In terms of levels there is a tutorial level and two main levels. The tutorial simply teaches the player the controls, provides some backstory, and introduces to the player to basic tasks.

The first main level introduces player to Robot NPCs, explosive boxes, and much more. The player has to figure out how to complete their tasks, and when they do, they destroy this part of the factory and unlock the next level. The final level is much larger, has a giant AI-controlled claw, metal boxes, and many small sorting robots (other B.E.E.P's). The player takes control of the claw, disrupts the BEEPs, and puts a metal box down the wrong chute, causing enough destruction to completely destroy the factory; completing the game.

The game was designed to have 10-15 minute playtime for new players with limited replayability. This was because the game would be presented to QUT industry partners who would not be able to play the game for long and so we wanted to make it as polished as possible.
