Project Environmental Threat

Environmental Threat is an educational mobile game made for Baringa State Primary School to educate its students about the endangered Wallum Sedge Frog. Our game became a part of their school curriculum.
Play (WebGL) Download
Gameplay
My Role
Envronmental Threat was made in Unity within a semester for my second Games Studio class. I played a programming role in a group of 4 with a producer, a designer/artist, and an animator.

The design for the game involved regular communication with a Baringa State High School representative, who told us what curriculum points the students had, and supplied us with material such as frog call audio.

As I was the main programmer of the group, I implemented most of the minigame functionality including:
  • Mobile touch and drag controls.
  • Menu buttons and scene transitions.
  • Game management and level progression.
  • Unity PlayerPrefs save system.
  • Score-to-colour lerping.
  • Particles and effect creation and spawning.
Game Summary
Environmental Threat was built as three minigame levels: one to teach students how to identify the frog's habitat, one to teach how their habitat is being affected (changes in pH), and one to identify its calls.
